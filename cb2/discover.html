History

Dallas, Texas, established in 1841, began its history as a humble frontier trading post. Its geographical location made it an attractive spot for settlers, leading to its incorporation as a city in 1856. The advent of the railroad in 1873 ushered in a significant growth phase, transforming Dallas into a bustling commercial and trading center. In the early 20th century, Dallas's economy expanded further due to the discovery of oil in Texas, drawing an influx of people and businesses. The city faced a historical challenge in 1963 with the assassination of President John F. Kennedy, an event that significantly impacted its national image. However, Dallas displayed resilience and, in the latter part of the 20th century, it emerged as a technological and telecommunications hub. Presently, it is one of the largest and most influential cities in the United States, distinguished by its robust economy, vibrant arts scene, cultural diversity, and significant contributions to numerous industries.

Demographic

Population:
According to the U.S. Census Bureau, as of 2020, Dallas has a population of about 1.288 million people.

Education:
Dallas is an educational hub, home to some of the best schools in Texas. According to a 2020 Niche rating, DFW [Dallas / Fort Worth] school districts accounted for seven of the top ten best school districts in Texas. The Dallas metropolitan area is also home to over 30 colleges.

Income:
The average annual household income in Dallas is $92,785, while the median household income sits at $58,231 per year. Residents aged 25 to 44 earn $62,633, while those between 45 and 64 years old have a median wage of $62,861.

Reuinion Tower

From 470 feet up you'll experience breathtaking 360-degree panoramic views and an indoor/outdoor observation deck that lets you see for miles in any direction. Day or night, the iconic Reunion Tower a must-see in Dallas.

Dallas World Aquarium

The Dallas World Aquarium is a for-profit aquarium and zoo located in the West End Historic District of Dallas, Texas, USA. It aids conservation and education by housing many animals that are threatened or endangered as part of a cooperative breeding program with other zoos around the world.

Chihuly Garden and Glass

The Dallas Farmers Market is a historical produce terminal for the state of Texas with an agricultural base. The historical context emphasizes the importance of farmers markets to the health of a community. Self-led and instructor guided tours available.

Nasher Sculpture Center

Opened in 2003, the Nasher Sculpture Center is a museum in Dallas, Texas, that houses the Patsy and Raymond Nasher collection of modern and contemporary sculpture. It is located on a 2.4-acre site adjacent to the Dallas Museum of Art in the Dallas Arts District.

JFK Memorial Plaza

The John Fitzgerald Kennedy Memorial is a monument to United States President John Fitzgerald Kennedy in the West End Historic District of downtown Dallas, Texas erected in 1970, and designed by noted architect Philip Johnson.
